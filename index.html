About Mohammed Adamu Abubakar

A Life of Service

Born in Lafia, Nasarawa State, Mohammed Abubakar Adamu has dedicated over four decades to public service and law enforcement. With a Bachelor's degree in Geography from Ahmadu Bello University and a Master's in International Criminal Justice from the University of Portsmouth, he combines academic rigor with practical leadership.

As the 20th Inspector-General of Police (2019-2021), he modernized Nigeria's policing, championed community engagement, and earned national honors including the Commander of the Order of the Federal Republic (CFR).

Global Experience

His international career at INTERPOL (1997-2007) made him the first African to serve as Director of NCB Services. He pioneered standards in economic and financial crime investigation, bringing world-class governance practices to Nigeria.

Now, he seeks to bring this wealth of experience, integrity, and vision to serve Nasarawa State as governor—ensuring security, transparency, and sustainable development for all citizens.

Key Achievements

A distinguished career marked by firsts, innovations, and unwavering commitment to excellence

🛡️

20th Inspector-General of Police

Served as Nigeria’s 20th Inspector-General of Police (2019–2021) One of his biggest achievements was rising through the ranks of the Nigeria Police Force to become the 20th Inspector-General of Police. During his tenure, he led national efforts to combat crime, kidnapping, banditry, and terrorism across Nigeria.

🌍

First African INTERPOL Director

Pioneered international policing standards and cooperation

Before becoming IGP, Adamu served at INTERPOL, where he:Led major international criminal investigations Became the first African to serve as a Director in INTERPOL’s history at the African Sub-Directorate. This elevated Nigeria’s presence in global policing and intelligence cooperation.

🏗️

Launch of Operation Puff Adder

He introduced Operation Puff Adder, a nationwide security operation aimed at tackling banditry, kidnapping, and armed robbery.

  • Arrest of over 1,500 kidnappers
  • Arrest of 2,600+ armed robbery suspects
  • Rescue of hundreds of kidnap victims
  • Recovery of thousands of firearms and stolen vehicles

    • This operation helped restore security in several hotspots including the Kaduna-Abuja corridor and parts of the North-West.

    🤝

    Police Welfare and Institutional Reformsr

    During his tenure, he implemented reforms aimed at improving professionalism and welfare in the police force:

  • Promotion of over 120,000 police personnel
  • Introduction of health insurance coverage for retired officers
  • Establishment of training institutions for police officers.

    Community Policing Champion

    Awarded for innovative community engagement and public safety

    He championed community policing reforms, encouraging citizens and communities to work closely with law enforcement to identify and address security threats. This approach strengthened collaboration between police, traditional leaders, and local communities.

    🪢

    Community Empowerment and Philanthropy in Nasarawa

    After retirement, he launched empowerment and social support programs in Nasarawa State that include:

  • Business grants and startup support for youths and women
  • Vocational training programs
  • Free medical outreach and healthcare services for vulnerable communities.
